Overview

The Mars Exploration mission is a joint effort between NASA and the European Space Agency (ESA) to explore the surface of Mars and gather scientific data. The mission involves several unmanned rovers, including the Perseverance rover, which landed on Mars in February 2021.

The mission's primary goals are to search for evidence of past or present microbial life on Mars, study the planet's geology and climate, and prepare for future manned missions to the planet.

Mission Timeline

  • 2011

    Launch of Mars Science Laboratory, which carried the Curiosity rover to Mars.

  • 2012

    Arrival of Curiosity on Mars. The rover began exploring the planet's surface and collecting data.

  • 2016

    Launch of the Mars InSight mission, which landed a stationary probe on the planet's surface to study its interior.

  • 2020

    Launch of the Mars 2020 mission, which carried the Perseverance rover to the planet's surface.

  • 2021

    Arrival of the Perseverance rover on Mars. The rover began exploring the Jezero Crater and searching for signs of past microbial life.
